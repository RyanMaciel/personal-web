Being a college student provokes a special kind of anxiety in many students, myself included. In addition to the growing pains of growing out and living on one’s own, and the financial pressures that many students in the U.S. face, the exposure to new knowledge exerts pressure on new students. Being a newly minted member of a community that contains a diverse group of students and professionals gives one an acute sense of just how motivated and focused those around them are. In additional to this immediate pressure, one can’t help but think about the thousands of other college students that could be competitors for internships or future employment. Each person passing in the quad corresponds a legion of similar people scattered across the world.

Many colleges choose a particular type of student. While in high school, a person's interests and academics could differentiate them from their peers, but when in college, they become one of potentially thousands who take the same classes and are on the same path. The pressure comes from a desire to differentiate again. The realization that there are hundreds or thousands who are just like you can be jarring.
